Beau Sloan

about me

I’m Beau Sloan, a driven undergraduate student at Brigham Young University pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Pre-Business with plans to major in Finance. With a 3.78 GPA and active involvement in the Finance Society and the Private Banking and Wealth Management Club, I’ve built a strong foundation in financial principles, economic analysis, and data-driven decision-making. My passion for investing and markets has led me to success in several competitive finance case competitions, including a second-place finish at the Brigham Capital Stock Pitch Competition, where I presented a valuation on Build-A-Bear Workshop.

My experience extends beyond the classroom into practical financial analysis. As a financial analyst at Brigham Capital, a student-run hedge fund with $190,000 AUM, I’ve conducted in-depth equity research, built DCF models, and pitched actionable investment ideas such as Datadog. In parallel, I serve as a student administrator for BYU’s Management Department, where I’ve optimized budget reporting processes for an $8 million annual budget and streamlined interdepartmental communications. My work experience also includes a design-focused role at Picture Perfect Construction, where I managed a $1.2 million landscaping project, blending creativity with logistical precision.

I’ve also gained leadership and interpersonal skills through volunteer service, including a two-year mission in Cape Town, South Africa, where I led diverse teams and implemented engagement strategies. Whether mentoring young athletes at the Provo Recreation Center or playing semi-pro hockey in Utah’s MHL League, I bring energy, discipline, and a commitment to personal growth to every environment I’m part of. I’m excited about a future in asset management, where I aim to combine analytical insight with a client-focused mindset to help others build and preserve wealth.
